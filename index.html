DIGITAL MUSEUM EXHIBIT

Portable music changed how people moved, listened, and felt ownership over sound.

Pocket Sounds is a curated exhibit about the shift from tapes to discs, from files to streams, and from physical collections to instant access. The site is designed as a museum experience, not just a timeline.

CURATOR NOTE

This exhibit asks one central question: when music became portable, what changed more — the technology, or the listener?

MUSEUM CONCEPT

What this museum is about

This museum explores the history of portable music technology through five turning points. Each artifact marks a different idea of freedom: carrying music, skipping tracks, shrinking files, storing thousands of songs, and streaming nearly everything.

Design Style

Swiss design: grid-based layout, strong typography, clean spacing, limited color palette.

Cialdini Principle

Curiosity: each section teases the next shift so users keep exploring.

Brand Archetype

Sage: informative, reflective, and educational in tone.

EXHIBIT FLOW

Five turning points in portable listening

1979

Sony Walkman TPS-L2

The Walkman turned private listening into a public lifestyle. Music was no longer tied to the living room or car stereo.

1984

Sony Discman D-50

The Discman made compact discs portable and pushed listeners toward cleaner, skip-forward digital convenience.

1995

The MP3 Name Arrives

Compression changed everything. Music became lighter, easier to copy, store, and move across devices and the internet.

2001

Apple iPod

The iPod combined portability with scale. Suddenly a pocket device could hold a large personal library.

2008

Spotify Launch

Streaming reframed ownership. Access became more important than carrying files, discs, or tapes.

VISITOR TAKEAWAY

The story is really about control.

Every stage of portable music gave listeners a new kind of power. The Walkman gave mobility. The Discman improved digital fidelity. MP3 compressed music into portable data. The iPod scaled a personal collection. Streaming traded ownership for convenience.
